Hiking the JMT requires a high level of physical fitness to ensure successful completion. The hike requires 3-4 weeks of hiking 10+ miles daily at elevations averaging around 10,000 ft above sea level. Inadequate preparation can lead to extreme fatigue, altitude sickness, and a failure to complete the trail.

Training Plan

Successful hikers focus on three core elements when preparing for the JMT. These are:

Distance
Hikers need to be able to cover long distances, with heavy backpacks.
Elevation
Each day of hiking covers large elevation gains, all at high altitude. This is a lot of climbing and training should reflect that.
Durability
Anyone can hike for 2-3 days straight. 3+ weeks requires assurance that your body is able to recover quickly and perform day after day.

More information on training as well as a full 16-week training plan can be found here.

The training plan includes running, short and long hikes, weights and plyometrics, as well as short overnight trips to gain comfort with gear.
